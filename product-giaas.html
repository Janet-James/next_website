Geographical Information as a Service – GIaaS

Conflux and amalgamation of geospatial technology enterprise solutions, analytical tools, etc., are excogitated one of the key drivers mounting the efficacy of geospatial analytics across diverse industries. Though Geospatial technology has been dilatory in adopting cloud computing, the benefits of cloud-based hosting services for spatial data and analysis are prominent. The Geospatial industry has been sluggish to embrace the trend due to the complex nature of GIS software and the types of analyses for which they are used. GIS on Cloud platform is anticipated as an ideal tool to upgrade conventional GIS applications and provide a broad spectrum of services to users across the globe. Today, with the emergence and advancement of cloud computing, there have been great evident milestones with numerous data sharing and analyses platforms emerging every now and then. The flexibility offered by Internet-based services has led more GIS users to take advantage and eventually the numbers of GIS users have tremendously increased. Delivery of services in flexible forms made the user to easily utilize the available functionality and perform tasks efficiently on time.

Although, Geospatial technology is used at various levels perspective of the Geospatial technology has not yet been explored owing to the following challenges

  • Non-availability of any standard policy with regard to the use of geo-data
  • Lack of awareness among the stakeholders regarding the use of geo-spatial data
  • Lack of qualified and trained manpower having expertise in Geospatial system
  • GIS initiatives are not extended in a collaborative and pervasive way
  • Collating and validating data is a cumbersome activity
  • Restrictions imposed does not allow public usage of Geospatial data/information
  • Effort duplication and lack of transparency
  • Non-availability of high-resolution maps
  • No standard mechanism for data integrity and updation
  • Lack of a collaborative and integrated Geo-Spatial platform

GIaaS - Geographical Information as a Service is NEXT's Geospatial Platform which is an Internet-based application providing shared and trusted geospatial data, services, and applications for use by the public and by government agencies and partners to meet their mission needs.

GIaaS is a “one-stop-shop” to deliver trusted, consistent data and services which provide access to authoritative data to support informed decision making as well as problem-solving applications (services) that are built once and used many times across multiple stakeholders. It is a shared cloud-computing infrastructure to host data and applications.

GIaaS has the ability to use visualization and mapping to help understand the impacts of events and how they affect citizens and government missions are very powerful. Whether it is a response to a natural disaster or viewing patterns of economic impact to our communities; visualizing where, when, and how these events occur lays a foundation for action and can help:

  • Put events in the context of location, environment, and people affected
  • Discover trends and relationships we might otherwise miss
  • Communicate more effectively through maps
  • Streamline geospatial data collection, delivery, visualization, and analysis
  • Distill large amounts of complex tabular and spatial information into user-friendly formats
GIAAS Objectives

To promote, facilitate, coordinate and support the development of a dynamic and flexible geospatial environment that will:

  • Facilitate information sharing across boundaries.
  • Protect information rights and privileges with hardened security and access levels.
  • Facilitate and ensure compatibility and interoperability through the systems
  • Enable decision-makers with a full range of geographic data and spatial analysis
  • Ensuring strategic business-driven deployment/acquisition of GIS related technologies and geospatial data to improve services and operations
  • Centralized administration of a GIS to ensure efficient use of data, applications, and GIS-related resources to reduce redundancy and duplications of efforts.
Geo-Information Display

Base Layer - is a pre-defined group of layers that draws continuously during Map navigation. By using a high-performance drawing engine, Base Layers significantly improve the display speed responsiveness of map, and provide a continuous visual context for data that you’re viewing, editing, or analyzing.

Map View - There are two types of Map views available: 2D map view & 3D map view. Toggling Map view switches from 2D to 3D map view & vice versa.

Location Overview - An overview map defines the boundaries of the main map within the context of its surrounding areas. It provides a generalized view of a geographic area.

Map Info – A set of tools used to show information about the map like scale, north, zoom level and coordinate information.

GeoSpatial Navigation
Map Navigation

The map is the core of the GIaaS. The map navigation includes Zoom In, Zoom Out, Pan, Full Extent, Center at, Zoom to Area of Interest, Zoom to Bookmark, Zoom to Scale are done using a mouse(zoom by rotating the wheel and pan by dragging). Touch devices support gestures for map navigation.

Feature Search

Finding a feature can be performed spatially or non-spatially. Spatial search (also called geospatial search) allows data that has a geographic location & enhance the search results by limiting them to a physical area. Non-Spatial search (also called attribute search) is the simple search based on their attributes.

Layer Tree

Also called Layer Manager or Table of Map Content, allows to manage displayed map layers like Layer visibility, Layer opacity, Layer re-order, zoom to layer, remove layer (limited to user added layer) and Layer Table.

External Layers

Function to parse user selected spatial file or WMS and load them over the main map. (Support available now for shapefile, kml, geoJSON file formats).

Identification

To get the details of the features, use the identify tool. By using the Identify tool, it identifies all layers in the main map by default. Layer Options like topmost layer, selected layer & all layers can be provided.

Bookmark

Provide shortcuts to user-defined places on a map. Map user can create them based on the current location and scale of the map. When clicked a bookmark, the map zooms to that location. Map user can also delete the bookmarks.

Measure

Provides basic mensuration like the length of a line & the area of a polygon over the map. Before or after performing each measurement, units of measure can be changed.

Spatial Analysis

Is a type of geographical analysis which seeks to explain geographic patterns of behavior and its spatial expression in terms of mathematics and geometry, that is, locational analysis. Our spatial analysis module includes Geocoding, Routing, and Buffering.

Charts on Map

Is a unique functionality of generating geotagged charts displayed over the map itself. Charts include – Choropleth Map, Heat Map, Proportional Charts – Bar charts, Pie charts, Doughnut.

Map Outputs

The map can be exported to PDF or as images or printed.

Redlining

Is a tool to highlight or put notes on the main map. GIaaS supports different styles of redlines (point marks, lines, squares or polygons & text entries) and attaches them the geospatial context.
