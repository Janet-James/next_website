SERVICE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

A Service Management System is a primary IT function within the discipline of IT service management (ITSM) as defined by the Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL). It is intended to provide a Single Point of Contact ("SPOC") to meet the communication needs of both users and IT staff or CAPS (Client Application Service & Support) and also to satisfy Customer requests. Some of the challenges faced without the service management system are lack of communication with the customers, lack of meeting the service level agreements, efficient schedules, keep track of the records, etc.,

The Service Management System will have the following features

  • “Request New Service” enables the user to raise their new service request.
  • Once the ticket is raised, the “SLA (Service Level Agreement)” will be effective.
  • Provides a one-place reference to view the logged defects by one and the support team.
  • Rich text or HTML email is supported and allows for rich text markup in staff replies and internal notes posted to the ticket thread.
  • Enables rule definition to route incoming tickets to the right departments or staff members, and action triggers.
  • Configurable help topics for web tickets. Route inquiries without exposing internal departments or priorities.
  • Ticket locking mechanism to allow staff to lock tickets during response and avoid conflicting or dual responses.
  • Assign tickets to teams/individuals to make sure it's being handled by the correct staff. Assign tickets to staff or to a team.
  • Automatic reply sent out when a new ticket is opened or a message is received.
  • Internal notes can be added to the tickets. Activity logs let to see the events or actions that have been taken, when they took place, and by whom.
  • SLA Plans allow you to track tickets and due dates without the hassle.

TICKET MANAGEMENT

A Ticket Management facilitates to raise new tickets, manages and maintains the list of issues.

  • Automate ticket management to facilitate service requests, SLAs, and ticket prioritization
  • Create, assign, track, manage, and integrate tickets for any number of custom issues
  • Implement strong and effective workflows for both internal and customer-facing support teams
Request New Service

Request new service enables to raise the new tickets. Help Topics will split the Ticket based on the selection from the drop-down list. Technical Support will be added based on Products. Report an issue or access issue facilitates based on Employee issues in the system or network connectivity or etc. Maintenance issue tracks the Issues related to the maintenance team. HR General Issue keeps track of inquiry to HR or HR Related Problem. Feedback ensures to collect each ticket feedback from the users.

Priority enables the users can confess the priority of their service request. The below issue details will be captured.

  • Issue Summary – Subject Line of the Issue to be resolved
  • Description – Details Summary of the issue to be resolved
  • Attachment – Any files with reference to the issue can be attached to the request
Email Ticketing

The service management enhances the users can request a service by sending mail to the service desk Mail Id, instead of using Web Service. The Email of particular mail will be converted as Ticket. Email and Name of the service management users are captured from email id name of the mail. The subject line will be converted as Issue Summary. The body of the content will be converted as Description of the ticket.

TICKETS VIEW & RESPONSE

The service calls view and response from each ticket can be maintained.

All Service Calls – Accessible to Service managers and Service team members

All Service Calls are categorized under states like
  • Opened – List of Opened Tickets
  • Answered – List of Answered Tickets
  • Resolved – List of Resolved Tickets
  • Verified – List of Verified Tickets
  • Closed – List of Closed Tickets
  • Overdue – List of Overdue Tickets

The Service Manager can view all the tickets under all the departments. The Service team members can able to see only the tickets that are dispatched to their respective departments. Under “All Service Calls” response form, the service team member or the service manager can do the actions/responses such as

  • Answering the opened/answered tickets
  • Resolving the opened/answered tickets
  • Closing the verified tickets
  • Assigning the tickets to other teams or any individual when answering the ticket
My Service Calls – Accessible to all the users

My Service Calls are categorized under states like
  • Opened – List of tickets opened by the logged in user
  • Answered – List of tickets in the answered state, opened by the logged in user
  • Resolved – List of tickets in resolved state, opened by the logged in user
  • Verified – List of tickets in the verified state, opened by the logged in user
  • Closed – List of tickets in the closed state, opened by the logged in user
Under “My Service Calls” response form, the users perform actions/responses such as
  • Answering the opened/answered/ resolved tickets
  • Verify the resolved tickets
  • Reopen the closed tickets

TICKET ASSIGNMENT

The service team member or the service manager can assign the ticket to other teams under DSS (Dedicated Service Support) teams and CAPS /PSS teams. It is also possible to assign the tickets to the individuals under each team.

Watching Ticket

The service Team member can choose their favorite ticket by using “star” on the particular ticket’s response form. Selected member will response that ticket whenever that particular ticket is opened.

Collision Detection

When two service team members open the same ticket, collision detection alert will send to service team members. After the alert, one service team member will work on the ticket. To avoid this mistake getting the same reply from the service team using service Team collision detection.

Adding Notes to particular Ticket

The Service team member can add notes based on any tickets about the idea they have in their mind. It will be useful for other service team members to respond to the ticket if they are not sure about the solution.

Check My Service Call Status

The Check service call status is for the users who raise the ticket. The user can check the ticket status of all the ticket raised by them is closed or opened. This facilitates the user to check the solution form the ticket already raised for the same problem.

SLA POLICIES

A service level agreement (SLA) policy lets you set standards of performance for your support team. You can set SLA policies for the time within which agents should respond to, and resolve tickets based on ticket priorities, and set up automatic escalation rules to notify specific agents about SLA violations.

Understanding SLA Policies

Customers want to know when they can expect a response and resolution from Service team when they submit a support request. Set, manage, and meet customer expectations by setting up service level agreements (SLAs) for support. With AXIS, Admin can define ticket priorities and requirements for resolution times so service team can know exactly which issues to address first and how quickly the customer expects a response. Every ticket in AXIS follows your SLA policy and the ‘due by’ time is driven by their priority. And when a ticket becomes overdue, the admin will make sure that the service team is notified of it. A service level agreement (SLA) policy set standards of performance for the support team.

Rebranding Help Desk

The rebranding is to influence a customer's perception about a product or service or the company overall by revitalizing the brand and making it seem more modern and relevant to the customer's needs. Changing the logo of AXIS can be done under General Settings in Settings and Management menu. It is accessible only to the Service Manager.

Knowledge Base

Even though maintaining and updating a knowledge base is pretty hard, a well-stocked knowledge base can be very beneficial for both agents and customers. The knowledge base is the pool of answers to frequently asked questions, can not only make sure that your agents are always up-to-date, but it also prevents your customers from raising tickets about the same thing over and over again. They can search for the answers themselves instead of turning to you for a solution every single time. This helps in reducing your support load and empowers your customers.

  • Well set up and updated knowledge base solves two of the biggest help desk problems
  • First, since all service team has a commonplace to pool in and share solutions, admin can be sure that customer responses are consistent throughout.
  • Second, since customers can access and help themselves to solutions, they feel more confident about the business.

AUTOMATIONS

Dispatcher

Dispatch Tickets Based on Help Topic and classification. Based on the user selection, the ticket will be segregated. This will helps to automate a process which involves many tickets that fall under similar criteria. Help Topic is for internal organization AXIS Users. Classification is for client login users of AXIS based on the project are tickets are classified.

Email Notifications

AXIS allows service team to send customers automated email notifications that will make sure that the users are always well-informed of the progress of their solution. AXIS can also send notifications to the service team to alert them of updates in the ticket or a new ticket in their queue. Escalation mail notifications are sent automatically to the Level 1, 2 & 3 service team members and service manager according to SLA Policies. When there is an SLA breach, it will be notified to the Service Manager as well as the particular Service Team.



Helpdesk Gamification

Ticket Survey / Rating & Feedback

The users who created the ticket are requested to rate their closed tickets before they create their next ticket in AXIS. They can also leave feedback to the ticket in the ticket survey form.

USER MANAGEMENT

Managing PSS (Product Service Support)

The users are added to PSS / CAPS to serve and support clients and their products and it can be managed only by the Service Manager. The PSS / CAPS team can be divided into level 1 support, level 2 supports and level 3 support for each product. The clients are invited under their respective product to allow them to request for service when are in need of PSS / CAPS team’s help. When the client raises a ticket, it will be assigned to the particular team and notified them to work on that issue.

Adding Multiple Email Address

AXIS adds an unlimited number of email addresses to helpdesk and makes it easy to manage all the support queries from one place. Similar to primary support email, all emails received in your additional mailboxes will also be converted into tickets automatically.

Supporting Multiple Products

Support multiple products from a single helpdesk without having to pay for any extra licenses, with AXIS. AXIS adds multiple products with respective service team members to serve and support the product.

SECURITY

Blocking Users

Service Manager can restrict support service team member and customers who can see support portal. Once the Service Manager enables block in the portal, those blocked users will not be able to use the AXIS.

Password Policy

Admin can set up different levels of password security using AXIS for service team members and customers. By default, any password must contain a minimum of 8 characters and must not contain the username.

Keyboard Shortcuts

AXIS helps to navigate the way around helpdesk easily by using keyboard shortcuts. These shortcuts can be used to perform repetitive tasks quickly and manage the properties of tickets on the fly.

Services Status (Maps)

The map shows pointers on the exact location where the tickets are raised. Based on the ticket status the colors of the map pin will be changed.
